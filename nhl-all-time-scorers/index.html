All Time Leading Scorers

Pictured below are the top 50 goal scorers all time in the NHL over the course of their career.

  • Bubble sizes represent number of goals.
    • Largest: Wayne Gretzkey at 894
    • Smallest: Dennis Savard at 473
  • Arcs represent the total goals by a team
    • Largest: Red Wings at 3,061
    • Smallest: Senators at 5
  • Chords represent the number of goals a player has scored for a team.
    • Largest: Gordie Howe for Red Wings at 786
    • Smallest: Multiple with 1

Hint: Hover over or click on teams, players and chords for a more detailed look. Keep mouse over for a toolbar. click to lock, again to unlock

Below are the top 600 scorers, while the graph is hard to read here, it is intersting to see how the teams with the most goals shift around. Notice how the Bruins moved from the 10th spot to the 2nd. And the Oilers dropped all the way down from 4th to 16th.

If you look at the Rangers and Red Wings, you will notice they have had many high career goal scorers play for them (center of the graphic). Actually, they've had a piece of 9 out of the 10 top goal scorers of all time. Mario Lemieux is the only player missing, who played his entire NHL career for the Penguins.

Additionally, if you focus in just on the Rangers, you will notice that they have had 6 out of 10 of them. Which is pretty crazy considering none of them scored the majority of their goals in New York. Talk about 1940 and fluke '94!
